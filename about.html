The Epic of Gilgamesh is one of the oldest literary works

Written between 2100 BC and 1200 BC, The Epic of Gilgamesh is one of the oldest and most influential literary works, inspiring classics such as The Illiad and The Odyssey. The Mesopotamian epic follows the adventures of the Gilgamesh, the God-King of Uruk. It was inscribed in Cuneiform script in clay tablets recovered and decyphered by archeologists starting in the 1850s.
